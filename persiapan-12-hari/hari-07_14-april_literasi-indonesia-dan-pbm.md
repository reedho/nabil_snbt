### Passage: Decision-Making (25 menit)

> Humans like to think of themselves as rational. However, decades of research reveal our choices are frequently influenced by cognitive biases — systematic deviations from rationality.
>
> The "anchoring effect": exposure to a number before estimating disproportionately influences judgment, even when irrelevant. In a classic experiment, participants estimated the percentage of African countries in the UN after spinning a random wheel. Higher numbers → higher estimates.
>
> "Loss aversion": the pain of losing something is psychologically ~twice as powerful as the pleasure of gaining something equal. This explains why investors hold losing stocks too long — avoiding the pain of realized loss.
>
> Governments leverage these insights through "nudge" policies — subtle changes in how choices are presented. Making organ donation the default (opt-out vs opt-in) dramatically increased donation rates in Austria and Belgium.

**1. Main argument:** (A) People are irrational (B) Biases affect decisions, understanding them leads to better outcomes (C) Anchoring is most important (D) Governments should control decisions

**2. "Leverage" means:** (A) ignore (B) criticize (C) utilize/take advantage of (D) invent

**3. "Nudge" policies work because:** (A) They force choices (B) They work with psychological tendencies (C) They eliminate biases (D) Only European
