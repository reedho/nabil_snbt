## PAGI (07:00 - 09:00) — Skimming, Scanning & 7 Tipe Soal

### 3 Teknik Wajib (15 menit)

**SKIMMING** — baca cepat untuk ide utama:
- Baca kalimat pertama tiap paragraf + kalimat terakhir paragraf akhir
- Target: 60-90 detik per passage 300 kata

**SCANNING** — cari info spesifik:
- Identifikasi kata kunci dari pertanyaan → scan passage → baca 1-2 kalimat sekitar
- Target: 15-20 detik per pencarian

**QUESTION-FIRST** — baca pertanyaan DULU:
```
SALAH: Baca passage → soal → passage lagi → jawab
BENAR: Baca soal → skim passage → jawab sambil scan
```

### 7 Tipe Soal — Ringkas (30 menit)

| Tipe | Ciri Pertanyaan | Strategi Kunci |
|---|---|---|
| **Main Idea** | "mainly about", "best title" | Harus mencakup SELURUH passage, bukan 1 paragraf |
| **Detail** | "according to", "the author states" | Scan kata kunci, jawaban = parafrase teks |
| **Inference** | "can be inferred", "implies" | Selangkah di luar teks, BUKAN lompatan jauh. Hindari always/never/all/none |
| **Vocabulary** | "closest in meaning" | SUBSTITUSI: ganti kata dengan tiap pilihan, mana yang cocok? |
| **Author's Purpose** | "why does the author mention" | Pikirkan MENGAPA, bukan APA |
| **Tone** | "author's tone/attitude" | Hafalkan: objective, critical, optimistic, skeptical, neutral, analytical |
| **Reference** | "'they' refers to" | Baca 1-2 kalimat SEBELUM kata ganti |

### Latihan Passage (45 menit)

> **The Digital Divide**
>
> In the 21st century, access to digital technology has become nearly as essential as access to clean water or electricity. Yet, a significant gap persists between those who have reliable internet access and the skills to use it effectively, and those who do not. This disparity, known as the digital divide, affects not only individual opportunities but also the economic development of entire nations.
>
> The digital divide manifests in several ways. The most obvious is the infrastructure gap: in many developing countries, broadband internet remains unavailable or unaffordable. According to the ITU, approximately 2.7 billion people worldwide still lack internet access. However, the divide extends beyond mere connectivity. Even in developed nations, disparities exist in digital literacy — the ability to effectively navigate, evaluate, and create information using digital technologies.
>
> Education has emerged as both a consequence and a potential solution. Students without internet access fell significantly behind during COVID-19 when schooling shifted online. Conversely, targeted digital literacy programs have shown promise. Estonia introduced coding education in primary schools in 2012, and now boasts one of the highest rates of digital literacy in the world.
>
> Addressing the digital divide requires coordinated efforts from governments, private sector, and international organizations. Simply providing devices is insufficient; sustainable solutions must include infrastructure development, affordability measures, and comprehensive digital education programs.

**1. Main idea?** (A) Internet should be free (B) Digital divide is complex, needs multi-faceted solutions (C) Developing countries need computers (D) COVID caused the digital divide

**3. "Manifests" means:** (A) hides (B) appears/shows itself (C) increases (D) disappears

**4. Inferred:** (A) All developed-country people have digital literacy (B) Devices alone solve the divide (C) Digital literacy involves more than internet access (D) Only poor countries affected

**5. Why mention Estonia?** (A) Small countries are better (B) Example of successful digital literacy initiative (C) Coding should be mandatory (D) Compare with Africa

**6. "Such" in last paragraph refers to:** (A) Inequalities (B) SDGs (C) Efforts including infrastructure, affordability, education (D) Companies

**7. Author's tone?** (A) Angry (B) Informative and concerned (C) Humorous (D) Indifferent
