### Simulasi Mini B. Inggris — 12 Soal, 12 Menit

**Passage I: Food Security**

> Climate change threatens global food security. Rising temperatures and extreme weather disrupt agriculture. The IPCC projects crop yields could decline up to 25% by 2050, even as population nears 10 billion.
>
> Effects are unevenly distributed. Tropical regions — many of the world's poorest — face the worst. In sub-Saharan Africa, where agriculture is ~60% of employment, smallholder farmers cannot afford drought-resistant seeds or irrigation.
>
> Paradoxically, the food system itself contributes to climate change — 10-12% of emissions directly, rising to 21-37% including land-use changes like deforestation.
>
> Solutions include agroecology, precision agriculture, and shifting toward plant-based diets in high-income countries.

**1.** Main idea: (A) Climate will end agriculture (B) Food security and climate are interconnected, need systemic solutions (C) Africa most affected (D) Everyone should be vegetarian

**2.** Crop yield decline projection: (A) 10% (B) 25% (C) 37%

**3.** "Paradoxically" suggests: (A) Simple situation (B) Agriculture both suffers from AND causes climate change (C) No effect (D) Info incorrect

**4.** Inferred about smallholder farmers: (A) Well-prepared (B) Prefer no tech (C) Disproportionately vulnerable due to limited resources (D) Don't produce much food

**Passage J: Biodiversity**

> Earth is experiencing its sixth mass extinction — unlike previous five (natural catastrophes), this one is driven by humans. Habitat destruction, pollution, overexploitation, invasive species, and climate change push species toward extinction at 100-1,000× the natural rate.
>
> Consequences extend beyond individual species. Ecosystems function through webs of interdependence — losing one species triggers cascading effects. Pollinating insects are essential for ~75% of food crops. Declining bee populations alarm scientists.
>
> Economic valuations of ecosystem services — benefits humans derive from nature — are ~$125 trillion/year, dwarfing world GDP. Yet these are taken for granted, a "market failure."
>
> Protected areas cover only ~17% of land. Scientists suggest 30% of land and ocean should be protected by 2030 — the "30 by 30" target.

**5.** What distinguishes the 6th extinction? (A) Slower (B) Caused by humans (C) Fewer species (D) Only marine

**6.** "Cascading" means: (A) improving (B) chain reaction (C) reversible (D) minor

**7.** "Market failure" refers to: (A) Financial collapse (B) Failure to value ecosystem services economically (C) Bankruptcy (D) Conservation failure

**8.** "30 by 30" is: (A) 30% species extinct (B) Conservation goal by scientific consensus (C) Current protection excessive (D) All solved by 2030

**Passage K: Remote Work**

> COVID-19 accelerated remote work transformation. Once a perk for few, it became necessity for millions overnight. Now organizations face a pivotal question: return to office, stay remote, or hybrid?
>
> A Stanford study found remote workers 13% more productive — fewer distractions, no commute. However, they report feeling less connected, and spontaneous collaboration is hard to replicate virtually.
>
> Cities relying on office workers experienced revenue declines. Conversely, suburban/rural areas saw influx of remote workers seeking affordable housing.
>
> The most enduring legacy may be rethinking what "work" means — replacing physical presence with focus on output and results. This paradigm shift can create a more flexible, inclusive workforce, provided digital access and worker isolation are addressed.

**9.** "Pivotal" means: (A) minor (B) crucial (C) temporary (D) financial

**10.** Remote workers more productive because: (A) Longer hours (B) Better tech (C) Fewer distractions, no commute (D) Higher salaries

**11.** Author's tone: (A) Supportive (B) Opposed (C) Balanced and analytical (D) Indifferent

**12.** Inferred from paragraph 3: (A) All cities suffered (B) Remote work has uneven geographic effects (C) Suburbs now more expensive (D) No economic effect
