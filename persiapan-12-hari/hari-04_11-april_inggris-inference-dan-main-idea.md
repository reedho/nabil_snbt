### Inference — Aturan Emas (10 menit)

> **Jawaban inference yang benar = selangkah di luar teks, BUKAN lompatan jauh.**

**Teknik:**
1. Baca pertanyaan → identifikasi paragraf relevan
2. Baca paragraf teliti
3. Eliminasi: ✗ tertulis langsung (itu detail) ✗ bertentangan ✗ terlalu ekstrem ✗ tidak didukung
4. Pilih yang **paling didukung** teks

### Latihan Inference Mini-Passage (30 menit)

**A:** "Japanese students rank among top performers in math/science. Their system emphasizes discipline and extensive after-school 'juku.' However, Japan reports some of the highest rates of student stress and anxiety."

**Q1.** Inferred: (A) Japanese students naturally smarter (B) Achievement may come at psychological cost (C) All attend juku (D) Stress leads to better performance

**B:** "Company revenue increased 15% last quarter, but stock dropped 8%. Analysts pointed to rising debt and CEO's sudden resignation as factors behind investor uncertainty."

**Q2.** About investors: (A) Don't care about revenue (B) Consider factors beyond revenue (C) Prefer lower revenue (D) CEO resignation was planned

**C:** "Despite billions in government subsidies, EV adoption remains limited in Southeast Asia. Infrastructure is a barrier: charging stations are scarce, electricity costs among highest in Asia."

**Q3.** Inferred: (A) SE Asians prefer gasoline cars (B) Subsidies alone insufficient for EV adoption (C) EVs will never succeed (D) Electricity is the only barrier

<details>
<summary>JAWABAN</summary>

Q1. **(B)** — prestasi tinggi + stress = ada "harga" psikologis
Q2. **(B)** — revenue naik tapi saham turun karena faktor lain
Q3. **(B)** — "despite billions in subsidies" + masih limited = subsidi saja tidak cukup
</details>

### Passage: Ocean Plastic (30 menit)

> Every year, ~8 million metric tons of plastic enter the oceans. From microscopic particles to massive debris, plastic pollution is one of the most pressing environmental challenges.
>
> Marine animals are visible victims. Sea turtles mistake bags for jellyfish. 90% of seabirds examined had plastic in their stomachs. When fish consume microplastics, toxic chemicals ultimately reach human dinner tables.
>
> Several countries have taken steps. The EU banned single-use plastics in 2021. Kenya imposed strict plastic bag bans. Meanwhile, innovative technologies are emerging: systems to collect ocean plastic using natural currents, and bacteria engineered to break down plastic.
>
> However, experts warn cleanup alone won't solve the problem. The fundamental issue is production volume — over 380 million tons annually — combined with inadequate waste management. A comprehensive solution must address production, consumption, and disposal simultaneously.

**Q4. Main idea:** (A) Sea turtles dying (B) EU banned plastics (C) Ocean plastic is severe, needs comprehensive solutions beyond cleanup (D) Developing nations are the source

**Q5. Best title:** (A) Death of Sea Turtles (B) Ban All Plastics (C) Ocean Plastic: A Crisis Demanding Action on Multiple Fronts (D) Bacteria That Eat Plastic

<details>
<summary>JAWABAN</summary>

Q4. **(C)** — mencakup masalah (par 1-2), upaya (par 3), kebutuhan solusi menyeluruh (par 4)
Q5. **(C)** — judul mencerminkan keseluruhan: krisis + butuh aksi komprehensif
</details>

### Passage: Social Media Paradox (30 menit)

> Social media was designed to bring people closer. Ironically, research suggests excessive use may increase loneliness. A 2021 study found young adults spending >2 hours daily on social media were twice as likely to report social isolation.
>
> Social comparison theory offers one explanation. When users scroll through curated feeds of peers' accomplishments and seemingly perfect lives, they compare these "highlight reels" to their own everyday reality, leading to diminished self-esteem.
>
> Additionally, screen time displaces face-to-face interactions, which are more beneficial for mental health. A text exchange cannot replicate the emotional richness of in-person communication — eye contact, physical touch, shared laughter.
>
> Some researchers caution against simplistic conclusions. Not all use is harmful. For those in remote areas or with limited mobility, online communities provide genuine connection. The key distinction: passive scrolling worsens well-being, while active engagement — meaningful conversations, sharing experiences — can enhance it.

**1. "Paradox" refers to:** (A) A platform type (B) Outcome opposite to intention (C) A theory (D) A disorder

**2. Author would agree:** (A) Ban social media (B) How people use it matters more than how much (C) Face-to-face is obsolete (D) Comparison only happens online

**3. "Highlight reels" implies:** (A) Complete picture (B) People only share best moments (C) Like sports (D) Photos are edited

<details>
<summary>JAWABAN</summary>

1. **(B)** — dibuat untuk menghubungkan tapi malah mengisolasi
2. **(B)** — "key distinction lies in HOW"
3. **(B)** — highlight reels = cuplikan terbaik saja
</details>
